## 1. Category Overview
### 1.1 Definition & Scope
Polyvinyl Chloride (PVC) Pipes are a versatile and widely used type of plastic pipe primarily for conveying water and other fluids. As a fundamental component in modern infrastructure, they are essential for plumbing, irrigation, and sanitation projects. The category primarily consists of pipes made from Unplasticized PVC (uPVC or PVC-U), a rigid and durable material ideal for pressure applications. Some variants are made from `[[PVC-Resin]]` that is UV-stabilized to prevent degradation from sunlight, a crucial feature for agricultural and surface applications (`pdf 1.json`, `pdf 4.json`).

The product scope is broad, covering everything from drinking water supply and agricultural irrigation to specialized uses. Key sub-categories include **uPVC Pressure Pipes** for water mains, **uPVC Column Pipes** designed for high-tensile loads in `[[Submersible-Pump]]` installations, **SWR (Soil, Waste, and Rainwater) Pipes** for drainage, and **Casing Pipes** for lining borewells (`pdf 2.json`, `pdf 9.json`). Manufacturers consistently highlight features such as corrosion resistance, smooth internal walls for high flow rates, low installation costs, and a long service life often exceeding 50 years (`pdf 10.json`, `pdf 3.json`, `pdf 4.json`).

## 2. Product Types & Variants
### 2.1 Main Product Types
PVC pipes are categorized based on their application, material properties, and jointing method.

-   **Unplasticized PVC (uPVC/PVC-U) Pressure Pipes**: The most common type, used for potable water supply, irrigation, and industrial fluid transport. They are classified by pressure ratings (kg/cm² or Classes) and conform to **IS 4985**. They are joined using `[[Solvent-Cement]]` or gasketed joints (`pdf 1.json`, `pdf 11.json`).
-   **uPVC Column Pipes**: Specialized high-tensile pipes designed as the riser pipe for `[[Submersible-Pump]]` installations in borewells. Their key features are high load-bearing capacity, precision-machined square threads, and locking mechanisms to prevent detachment. They are typically available in "Standard" and "Heavy" grades to suit various pump depths and weights (`pdf 9.json`).
-   **SWR (Soil, Waste, and Rainwater) Pipes**: Designed for gravity-fed drainage systems in buildings, conforming to **IS 13592**. Not suitable for pressure applications.
    -   **Type A**: Used for ventilating stacks and rainwater harvesting.
    -   **Type B**: Have thicker walls for higher strength, used for discharging soil and waste (`pdf 2.json`).
-   **Schedule Pipes (Sch 40, 80, 120)**: Classified per **ASTM D1785**, these pipes have a standard outside diameter for a given size, but wall thickness increases with the schedule number, enabling higher pressure tolerance. Common in industrial applications and plumbing (`pdf 12.json`, `pdf 4.json`).
-   **Clear PVC Pipes**: A transparent variant, typically with Schedule 40 dimensions, used in applications requiring visual monitoring of fluid flow, such as sight glasses, lab work, and low-pressure process lines (`pdf 5.json`).
-   **CM & CS Casing Pipes**: Designed specifically for borewell construction, conforming to **IS 12818**. CM (Casing Medium) is for medium-depth wells, and CS (Casing Shallow) is for shallower wells (`pdf 1.json`, `pdf 7.json`).
-   **PVC Agriculture (Agri) Pipes**: Intended for irrigation and agricultural pump sets, typically ISI marked and specified by diameter and a "kg" pressure rating (`call 16.json`, `IS 12231`).
-   **PVC Conduit Pipes**: Used as a protective casing for `[[Electrical-Wire]]` systems in construction (`call 19.json`, `call 22.json`).

### 2.2 Classification by Joint Type
-   **Plain End / Selfit (Solvent Weld)**: Pipes with either straight ends (Plain) oder one factory-formed socketed end (Selfit). Both are permanently joined using `[[Solvent-Cement]]`. Selfit pipes can reduce installation time and `[[Solvent-Cement]]` consumption by up to 50% (`pdf 7.json`).
-   **Bell End / Ringfit (Gasketed)**: Pipes with one flared "bell" end containing a groove for a `[[Rubber-Gasket]]`. This creates a push-fit, leak-proof seal that accommodates thermal expansion and contraction, common in SWR and underground water mains (`pdf 10.json`, `pdf 2.json`).

### 2.3 Available Sizes & Configurations
-   **Diameter**: The range is extensive, from small plumbing lines to large water mains.
    -   **Buyer Requests (common sizes)**: 25mm (~1"), 75mm (~3"), 100mm (4"), 125mm (5"), 200mm (8"), 250mm (10"), 315mm (12").
    -   **Manufacturer Offerings**: Standard Outside Diameters (OD) range from 20mm up to 400mm (`pdf 1.json` - `pdf 12.json`). Column pipes are available in bore sizes from 25mm (1") to 100mm (4") (`pdf 9.json`).

-   **Colors**: **Grey** is the most common colour for uPVC pressure pipes, followed by **White**. SWR pipes are typically grey. Other colors like **Blue** can be supplied upon request. Clear pipes are transparent (`pdf 10.json`, `pdf 4.json`).
-   **Quantity**: Procured by length ("meters"), units ("pieces"), or "bundles".

### 3.2 Specification Interdependencies
-   **Pressure, Wall Thickness, and Cost**: For a given diameter, a higher pressure rating (e.g., Class 3 / 6 kg/cm²) necessitates a thicker wall, which increases the material weight and a pipe's per-meter cost (`call 16.json`, `pdf 8.json`).
-   **Column Pipe Grade & Performance**: A "Heavy" grade column pipe has a thicker wall than a "Standard" grade of the same diameter. This results in a significantly higher Ultimate Breaking Load, Safe Pulling Load, and allows it to support a greater Pump Delivery Head, making it suitable for deeper borewells (`pdf 9.json`).
-   **SWR Type A vs. Type B**: For SWR drainage pipes, Type B has a thicker wall than Type A of the same diameter, providing the necessary strength for carrying soil and solid waste, while Type A is sufficient for rainwater and ventilation (`pdf 2.json`).
-   **Joint Type & Application**: Ringfit (gasketed) joints are preferred for buried non-pressure lines (like main drains) as they handle soil movement and thermal expansion. Selfit (solvent weld) joints form a rigid, monolithic structure ideal for above-ground plumbing and pressure lines.

### 8.3 Storage
> Proper storage is crucial to maintain pipe integrity and prevent damage. Based on industry best practices [Web]:
> -   **Support**: Store pipes on a level, flat surface. Use horizontal wooden battens spaced no more than 1 meter apart to prevent sagging. Do not store directly on uneven ground.
> -   **Stacking**: Stack pipes in layers, with larger diameters at the bottom. To prevent deformation of the lower layers, the stack height should not exceed 1.5 meters.
> -   **Protection**: Protect pipes from direct sunlight to prevent UV degradation, which can make them brittle. If stored outdoors, cover the stack with a light-colored, opaque tarp that allows for air circulation. Avoid dark tarps that absorb heat.
> -   **Handling**: Avoid dropping pipes or dragging them on rough surfaces. Keep them away from sharp objects, hot surfaces, and heavy machinery.
> -   **Inventory**: Practice "First-In, First-Out" (FIFO) to ensure older stock is used first.

-   **Minimum Order Quantity (MOQ)**: Mismatch between supplier's bulk MOQ and buyer's smaller requirement.
-   **Information Lag**: Delays in receiving a rate list or catalogue can lead buyers to seek other suppliers.

| **Selfit Joint** | A solvent weld joint where one pipe end is pre-socketed, eliminating the need for a separate coupler. |
| **Square Threads** | A robust screw thread profile with a square cross-section, used in high-load applications like column pipe couplers for superior strength. |
| **SWR** | Soil, Waste, and Rainwater. A category of non-pressure pipes used for gravity-fed drainage in buildings. |
| **uPVC** | Unplasticized Polyvinyl Chloride. A rigid, durable form of PVC ideal for pressure pipes and structural applications. |
| **UV Stabilized** | Indicates the material contains additives to protect it from degradation caused by ultraviolet (UV) light from the sun. |
| **Vicat Softening Temp.**| The temperature at which a plastic begins to soften rapidly, indicating its heat resistance. |
